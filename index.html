What Is Support Coordination?

Support coordination helps you to make the best use of your supports in plan.
Support coordination is a capacity building support which helps you to:

Understand and use you NDIS plan to pursue your goals and dreams.

Connect you with NDIS providers, community, mainstream and other government services.

Build your confidence and acquire skills to use and coordinate your supports.

Our Support Coordinators help you bring your NDIS Plan to life! We help you choose what are the best services and providers for you.

Three Levels of Support Coordination

Support Connection

This support helps to build your ability to connect with informal, community and funded supports.

Coordination of Supports

This level of support will assist you to build the skills to understand, implement and use your plan.

Specialist Support Coordination

This is a higher level of support for people with more complex and specialist support.

What do Support Coordinators do?

  • Help you to understand and implement your funded supports in your plan.
  • Link you to NDIS providers, as well as other community and government services.
  • Helping you to get the most out of your NDIS plan and ensure your funded supports are in line with your needs and preferences.
  • Assist you to build your ability to exercise choice and control.
  • Assist you to negotiate with providers about supports and services.
  • Collaborate and develop service agreements between you and your providers.
  • Build your capacity so you are independent and empowered to coordinate your own supports and participate in your community.
  • Assist you with planning and preparing your NDIS plan review.
